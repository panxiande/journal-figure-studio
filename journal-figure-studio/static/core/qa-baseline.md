# QA Baseline

Use this checklist for all modes. In draft mode, flag issues and proceed when they do not block a useful first figure. In submission mode, treat unknown official requirements as unresolved risks.

## Scientific Logic

- Core conclusion is visible in the panel order.
- Every panel has a key message and evidence role.
- No decorative or redundant panel is included solely to look complete.

## Statistics

- Show raw points or distributions when possible for small or moderate n.
- State n, biological versus technical repeat, center value, error definition, test, correction, and comparison set.
- Avoid p-value stars without a legend or method.
- Do not imply confirmation from exploratory plots.

## Image Integrity

- Microscopy, histology, gels, and blots have source paths, crop notes, processing notes, and scale bars where relevant.
- Brightness/contrast/gamma adjustments are global and disclosed when they affect interpretation.
- Splices, reused regions, pseudocolor, zoom crops, and composite channels are labelled.
- Do not generate or alter scientific evidence images with generative AI.

## Layout and Typography

- Final-size text is readable and non-overlapping.
- Panel labels match journal profile case, weight, and position.
- Axes, legends, scale bars, and annotations do not obscure data.
- Captions, titles, author names, and long method text are not embedded in the figure unless needed by venue.

## Export

- SVG/PDF text remains editable unless a profile requires outline text.
- Raster elements meet dpi expectations at final print size.
- Colors are RGB/grayscale unless a profile requires otherwise.
- Color encoding is accessible and does not rely only on red versus green.
